Why Learn With Us?

Pre-Reading Skills

Listening to alphabets lays the foundation for pre-reading skills, as learners become familiar with the sounds that letters make. This can contribute to the development of essential skills needed for decoding and understanding written words later on.

Accent

Learning alphabets through listening allows learners to pick up on pronunciation and accent nuances, which can be particularly important in languages with distinct phonetic variations.

Auditory Discrimination

Listening to the sounds of each alphabet helps develop phonemic awareness, which is the ability to identify and manipulate individual sounds in words. This is a crucial skill for early literacy development.
